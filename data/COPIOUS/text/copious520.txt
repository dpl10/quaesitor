Sepals imbricate, broadly ovate, obtuse or subacute, 3 mm long, 2.5 mm wide, densely pubescent, the three outer ones inclosing the two inner. Petals 8 mm long, 3.5 mm wide, obtuse. Stamens 15, in two series, the filaments broad, 1 mm long; anthers broadly ovate, 0.6 mm long, the appendix to the connective slender, as long as the anther. Ovary pubescent, the stylopodium very obscure or wanting. Style slender, 1.5 mm long. In the fruit all the sepals are accrescent, the three outer ones being 4.5 cm long, and 8-10 mm wide, obtuse, the two inner ones about 2 cm long or less, and 3 mm wide. The name tanguile is often applied to other plants which have soft or moderately hard red wood, whether they belong to this family or not. In the provinces of Bulacan, Tayabas and Mindoro, the name tanguile is often applied to Illipe ramiflora Merr., of the Sapotaceae.
